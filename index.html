Part Four

The River

The Trinity is the whole point. Late June water runs cold and clear — when temperatures hit 95°F, the river is where everyone ends up. Water shoes are non-negotiable; the rocks bake in the sun.

Kimtu Beach on the Trinity River, Willow Creek
Check Flows Before River Days Lewiston Dam upstream controls the Trinity's level. Summer baseflow is around 450 cfs but the Bureau of Reclamation can increase releases with little notice — when they do, the river can rise significantly within hours. Check current flows at waterdata.usgs.gov (search "Trinity River Salyer") the morning of any river day. If flows look elevated, stick to calmer spots.

Canyon Creek Trail, Trinity Alps Wilderness

Trinity Alps & Inland (~40–60 min east on Hwy 299)

Best Hike Canyon Creek Trail to the Falls ▼
~40 min from house~9 mi RT to fallsHard
★ 4.8 (1,077 AllTrails reviews · Hard · 15.6 mi · 2,942 ft gain)

The standout hike of the trip. Drive Hwy 299 east to Junction City, then Canyon Creek Road north 13 miles to the trailhead. The canyon is massive white granite, the waterfalls are genuinely dramatic — Lower Falls at mile 3.9, Middle Falls at 4.9. One of the best day hikes in Northern California.

Start by 8am. The switchbacks around mile 2.5 are fully exposed — brutal after 11am. Bring at least 2L of water per person. Don't leave food in the car; bears break into vehicles at this trailhead regularly.

Shorter option: Turn around at Lower Falls for an excellent ~8 mi day.

Part Seven

Other Things to Do

Beyond the river and the trails, the area has more going on than most people expect.

Fishing Trinity River — Right at the House ▼

Late June is prime for Spring Chinook Salmon — the river opens July 1 for salmon from the South Fork upstream. Year-round open for hatchery steelhead and trout. Closest access points: Tunnel Flat (6 mi east on 299) and Hawkins Bar (10 mi east).

Buy your CA fishing license before leaving SF — hard to find supplies in Willow Creek. Get it at ca.wildlifeapps.ca.gov or any sporting goods store. Best times: before 10am or dusk — fish go quiet in the midday heat.

Night Sky Stargazing from the House ▼

Salyer sits in a genuine dark sky zone with zero light pollution. The new moon is June 25 — by June 29 you'll have just a thin crescent, meaning near-perfect conditions all three nights. Step outside after 10pm and the Milky Way should be fully visible.

Pack a blanket. The driveway or porch both work. This is one of those things you don't plan and end up talking about.

Day Trip Arcata & Trinidad — Redwood Coast ▼
~50 min west on Hwy 299

Hwy 299 to the coast is wide and well-graded — not the cliff roads you wanted to avoid. Arcata Community Forest has easy redwood trails right behind town. Then 20 minutes north on flat Hwy 101 to Trinidad — dramatic sea stacks, and clam chowder at Trinidad Bay Eatery & Gallery on the bluff.

Part Eight

Field Notes

The stuff a local would tell you before you headed out. Not meant to alarm — just worth knowing.

🌊 The River Has Moods The Trinity is dam-controlled. Lewiston Dam upstream can increase releases with short notice, raising the river level noticeably within hours. Check flows the morning of any river day at waterdata.usgs.gov — search "Trinity River Salyer." If the river looks higher or faster than expected when you arrive at a swimming hole, trust your eyes. The locals know: when in doubt, watch the water a while before jumping in. Cold water + unexpected current is a combination to respect.
🌊 River Safety Basics The Trinity has had drownings, including at Kimtu. The current can be deceiving — it looks calmer than it is in places. Never swim alone. If you get swept into fast current: don't fight it, don't stand (the force will knock you under). Roll onto your back, feet pointing downstream, toes up, and look ahead to steer around rocks with your feet. Most incidents happen when people underestimate the cold, the current, or combine alcohol with swimming. You'll be fine if you're paying attention.
🔥 Wildfire Smoke Late June in Trinity County is peak fire weather — hot, dry, low humidity. Fires have burned directly along Hwy 299 in past seasons. It doesn't mean there will be a fire on your trip, but it's worth knowing. If smoke shows up, check airnow.gov or the PurpleAir app before Canyon Creek day. AQI in the orange or red isn't a great day for a long exposed hike. The house has WiFi so you can check once you arrive even without cell service.
🌿 Poison Oak Abundant in the Trinity River canyon — particularly along creek banks, in lower trail sections, and at river access points. It grows as a shrub with three shiny, slightly waxy leaves (green in summer, can have a reddish tinge). If you brush against it, wash the exposed skin with cold water and soap within 5–10 minutes — the oil (urushiol) is what causes the rash, and it takes a little time to bond to your skin. Tecnu or rubbing alcohol work well as a backup. Wear long pants on hikes into brushy areas.
🐍 Rattlesnakes & Ticks Both present in the area in summer. Rattlesnakes favor warm exposed rocks and granite — relevant on the upper sections of Canyon Creek where you're scrambling. Never reach blindly under rocks or logs; step on top of logs, not over them. Ticks are more common in brushy, shaded areas. Do a full check after any hike — behind knees, armpits, hairline. Permethrin spray on hiking clothes before you go is highly effective.
☀️ Heat Salyer regularly hits 95–100°F in late June. The rhythm locals use: river or hike in the morning, rest or nap during peak heat (1–4pm), then head back out in the early evening when it softens. Canyon Creek Trail specifically — if you start at noon you'll regret the switchbacks. Early start is not optional on hot days.
⛽ Gas Fill up in Redding before hitting 299. There's a station in Willow Creek but prices run high. If you're doing the Canyon Creek day trip east toward Weaverville, fill up in Willow Creek before you go — don't assume you can get gas out there easily.
🏥 Nearest Hospital Mad River Community Hospital, Arcata — about 50 miles west, roughly 1 hour on Hwy 299. No urgent care in Willow Creek. Trinity County Sheriff: (530) 623-2611. In an emergency, call 911 — response times are longer out here than in the city, so knowing this in advance is useful. WiFi calling from the porch should work for emergency calls.
🎣 Fishing License Required for the Trinity. Buy online at ca.wildlifeapps.ca.gov or any sporting goods store before you leave SF — supplies are hard to find in Willow Creek. The river opens July 1 for Chinook salmon in the lower section. Year-round for steelhead and hatchery trout.

River Gear

  • Water shoes / Chacos / Tevas — rocks are scalding in July, mandatory
  • Waterproof dry bag for keys, phone, wallet
  • Sunscreen SPF 50+
  • Wide-brim hat + polarized sunglasses
  • Quick-dry towels
  • Inner tubes optional — can buy at Ray's or Six Rivers Rafting in Willow Creek

Hiking

  • Hiking boots or trail shoes — loose rock near top of Canyon Creek
